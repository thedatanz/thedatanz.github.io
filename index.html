What?

The trusted open data source for people answering our country's toughest questions.

Why?

We are sick of downloading CSVs and making them ready for analysis. As users of data, it is incumbent on us to lead by example and demonstrate good practice.

"Data scientists spend 80% of their time cleaning data and 20% complaining about cleaning data." @vagabondjack #fintech #funny #analytics

— Kat Budd (@kat_budd) September 7, 2015

To put it bluntly, we should turn that 20% in to something useful that helps alleviate this problem.

How?

On the supply side, we will work with government agencies (and anyone else!) to help them open up data for the good of all. This means sharing resources that help agencies up-skill and close the resourcing gap between "we made some data" and "you can have it".

On the demand side, we will build tools for a wide variety of use cases and software. Data probably won't answer your tough questions if you need unfamiliar tools to get it.

When?

Here is our draft roadmap, which will change subject to our volunteers' availability.

  • Q2 2015 - Provide an open database of open data for GovHackNZ (failed!)
  • Q4 2015 - Gather volunteers and clarify objectives of thedata.nz
  • Q1 2016 - Launch browseable, downloadable time series open data store
  • Q2 2016 - Commence work with government agencies to build their open data capability
  • Q3 2016 - Launch time series API and open-source tools for using it
  • Q4 2016 - Begin work on next capability (perhaps GIS, perhaps network data)

If you have ideas of what else we should be doing, get in touch!
